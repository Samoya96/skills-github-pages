Fargo

Overview of the City

Located in southeastern North Dakota and often referred to as the Heartbeat of North Dakota, Fargo is renowned as one of the best places to live in the state. It is the most populous city in North Dakota and forms the border between Minnesota and North Dakota. The city of Fargo was founded in 1871 and has a rich history and vibrant culture. With landmarks like North Dakota State University, Fargo Air Museum and Fargodome, Fargo is a dynamic urban community. (Source: North Dakota Tourism Division)

Demographic and Economy

Fargo is home to over 133,000 residents and has around 56,890 households, averaging 2.14 persons per household. According to the 2022 American Community Survey, Fargo's demographic is comprised of a significantly larger White (Non-Hispanic) population approximating 102,000 residents. This is followed by 10,800 Black or African American (Non-Hispanic) residents and 5,120 Asian (Non-Hispanic) residents, making these the second and third largest ethnic groups, respectively. The city's median household income stands at $64,432, with approximately 13.3% of residents living at or below the poverty line. (Source: Data USA)

Popular Attractions

  • West Acres Mall - Offers a variety of stores, dining, and entertainment options.
  • Fargo Air Museum - Showcases a wide range of historical aircraft and aviation exhibits.
  • Plain Arts Museum - Features contemporary and traditional art from the region and beyond.
  • Red River Zoo - Home to over 30 species of animals.
  • Lindenwood Park - Enjoy trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas, ideal for family outings.
  • Fargodome - Attend major concerts, sports events, and other performances in this versatile arena.
  • Hjemkomst Center - See highlights of Scandinavian heritage, including a full-scale replica of a Viking ship.
