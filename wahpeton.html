Wahpeton

Overview of the City

Wahpeton is a small city located in southeastern North Dakota. It was established in 1869 by Morgan T. Rich, the first settler in the town. Wahpeton is regarded as a suburban town that blends the charm of country living with the conveniences of city life. This industrial area hosts numerous manufacturing and agricultural plants that boosts its local economy. Furthermore, due to its proximity to Fargo, it offers its residents easy access to extensive retail and entertainment options. (Source: City of Wahpeton)

Demographic and Economy

Wahpeton's population is around 8,000 people as of 2022, with a median age of 34 and a median household income of $51,417. Wahpeton, ND, employs around 4,320 people, reflecting a workforce engaged in various sectors such as agriculture, manufacturing, education, healthcare and mining. (Source: Data USA)

Popular Attractions

  • Chahinkapa Zoo - See several species of animals including an orangutan, bison, kangaroos, camels, zebras, and its newest attraction, white rhinos.
  • Bois de Sioux Golf Course - Visit the only golf course in the United States to feature 18 holes in two states, as it expands across the Minnesota border.
  • Richland County Historical Museum - Dive into the history of the area with fascinating exhibits and artifacts.
  • Red Door Art Gallery - Enjoy works by local artists and participate in art events and workshops.
  • Sculpture Garden - Enjoy the artistic displays and visually captivating outdoor setting.
